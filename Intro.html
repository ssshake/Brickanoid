New to Game Development?


Stick with game Tropes that you already know work:

    • Game Mechanics

    • Rules

    • Core Concept

    • Reward System


BECAUSE:


  • You have a reference point to how the finished product should behave.

    • If something isn't working as expected you know it's a design flaw, not a conceptual issue

  • You don't get 30 hours into game design before deciding that your product sucks

  • You have a good chance of being able to ship your first game while learning as you go.

  • You're scope is only to learn at this point, not to innovate



Borrow Assets:

  • 3D Models

  • Textures/Images

  • Sound Effects

  • Music


BECAUSE:


  • You don't want to impede your education by having to learn multiple disciplines at once.

  • Time vs Pay-off

  • Your first few games are possibly going to hit the trash can.

    • Don't invest too much time on projects while you're learning.

    • Fail fast, you'll learn better ways and use them later


BUT:


  • Borrow, don't steal

    • Give credit to the sources

    • Make sure you aren't infringing on their licenses (especially if you sell your games)

    • Consider open-sourcing your code, contribute back.



Borrow Code:

  • Plenty of good discussions and examples on the Unity forums

  • See how experienced people approach the same problem

  • Exposure to options you might not know about or have considered
